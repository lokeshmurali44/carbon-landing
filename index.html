Carbon Credit Tokenization FAQ

Find answers to common questions about carbon credits, verification, tokenization, and market participation.

Tokenized carbon credits represent verified carbon offsets (1 token = 1 tonne CO₂). These tokens are recorded on blockchain, enabling transparent ownership, traceability, and seamless transferability across global markets.

Carbon credits are verified through globally recognized registries such as Verra, Gold Standard, and other certification bodies that validate carbon offset projects.

Each verified carbon credit is minted as a digital token on the blockchain, where 1 token equals 1 tonne of CO₂ offset. This ensures precise tracking and transferability.

Carbon credits generate value through market demand, regulatory compliance requirements, asset appreciation, and yield from carbon offset activities.

You can trade carbon credit tokens on the marketplace or retire them to permanently offset your carbon footprint, with full on-chain verification.

All carbon credits are registry-backed and blockchain-tracked for full transparency.
